About

From medical AI to how machines see the world.

I'm a Software Engineering student at UNSW who likes turning messy, real-world problems into reliable software: backends, data pipelines, and cloud systems that hold up under real workloads.

My research curiosity started in a during my research assistantship at UNSW's School of Optometry & Vision Science. While building a deep-learning system for early glaucoma detection, it became obvious that you can't just deploy a black box when a patient's vision is on the line. Transparency and trust in AI genuinely matter.

One question kept nagging me: “why should we trust these models?” But you can't really trust something you don't understand, and to understand a model you have to know what it's actually doing inside. So the question deepened into a far more interesting one: “how do these models actually see the world?”

That curiosity led to my Honours thesis on multimodal convergence: testing whether vision models and language models, trained completely separately, still land on the same internal representation of a concept, and what pushes them to agree. I'm putting the Platonic Representation Hypothesis (and its refinement, the Aristotelian one) to the test.
